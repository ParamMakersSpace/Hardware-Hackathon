# HackWire – Hardware Hackathon 1.1

> **Engineering Principles of Life into Hardware**

HackWire is a philosophy-driven hardware hackathon where participants translate abstract principles of life into working hardware systems. It is not a conventional hackathon success here depends as much on conceptual clarity as on technical execution.

## 🧠 Theme: Tattva

HackWire was built around **Tattva** — fundamental principles of life drawn from the *Dakshinamurti Stotra* by Adi Shankaracharya. Participants were challenged to engineer these principles into physical hardware systems.

The eight Tattvas:

1. The World is a Reflection
2. Order Hidden in Chaos
3. The Tool Limits the View
4. Universal Controller
5. Superimposition
6. Bliss is the Background
7. The Unchanging Self
8. Maaya (One as Many)

## 🔧 Projects Built

Teams rapidly prototyped across multiple disciplines:

- EEG-based drone control systems
- Robotics Orchestra with synchronised motion and audio
- Gamified focus and cognition systems using EEG signals
- EEG-based game controllers
- CubeSat prototypes with manoeuvring and attitude-control concepts
- Wi-Fi based person detection systems
- PID control of a Robot Arm via IMU Sensor

## 🛠️ Hardware & Infrastructure

~**1,800 components** were issued across the event, including:

- Microcontrollers and single-board computers
- AI vision and camera modules
- Sensors — IMU, EEG, ECG, EMG, GSR, proximity, environmental
- Actuators, motors, servos, and drivers
- Drone kits and robotic platforms
- Communication modules — Wi-Fi, LoRa, GPS, NRF
- Audio-visual modules and displays
- Power systems, batteries, converters, and accessories

All components were **sponsored by Mythic Society** and sourced through **Robu**, **Robocraze**, and **PCB Cupid**.
